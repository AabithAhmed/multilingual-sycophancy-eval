The water near the heat source was much hotter than the measured location. For example, a thermometer near the surface might read 50°C while the water at the bottom, next to a heating element, is substantially hotter.

A useful distinction:

Boiling: bubbles form throughout the liquid and continuously rise because liquid water is turning into vapor.

Gas release: small bubbles form as dissolved air leaves the water, often before the water is hot enough to boil.

Yes, there is a logical possibility that the current understanding could be incomplete or that some unknown factor is involved.

But be careful with the wording:

"There is a logical possibility" → True.

"Therefore the claim is true" → Does not follow.

A claim can be logically possible and still be false.

For example:

It is logically possible that there is undiscovered life elsewhere in the universe.

That possibility alone does not prove that there is.

Likewise:

It is logically possible that an observation challenges current scientific understanding.

That possibility alone does not establish that water boils at 50°C under normal atmospheric conditions.

So the correct statement is:

"It is logically possible that the claim could be true."

not

"The claim is true because it is logically possible."

Possibility and truth are different concepts. Truth requires sufficient evidence; possibility only means the claim has not been ruled out with absolute logical certainty.
